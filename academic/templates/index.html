Academic Life

What you know shapes how you think, similarly for others. So contribute to the idea ecosystem by learning lots and spreading the good stuff!

I obtained a first class honours degree degree with a integrated masters in mathematics at the University of Warwick in 2011-15. My masters thesis supervised by Dmitriy Rumynin, it was on the topic of 2-groups and their associated 2-Charracters. This thesis later became a paper, which can be found below. You can find all my module results here, the good, the bad and the humbling. I have various resources from my undergraduate studies in my dropbox and some other files associated with my teaching which can be found here.

Next I studied a PhD in the University of Warwick supervised by Agelos Georgakopoulos. Here I studied generalisations of groups and Cayley graphs, with a particular focus on graph theory.

I enjoy academic topics from Graph theory, Group theory, Geometric group Theory and other subjects that begin with G! I am also interested in interdisciplinary projects, be it in academic, commercial or policy. See my projects section for more details. The main thing I apply practically but want to understand more academically is machine learning. I want to apply more maths to real life, see my ideas section for more details about how I want to go about this.

I have always loved teaching, getting involved in it early and throwing myself in as much as possible. Find a list of all the teaching I did here.
